Web Development Portfolio

I am a public policy professional who also builds websites. I educated myself in web design and development so I could maintain a site for the US Department of Veterans Affairs, and in the process discovered a new interest. While I don't have the credentials of a professional web developer, I learn quickly and enjoy solving problems.

The following are three examples of my past work...

VA Center for Innovation

One of my many responsibilities during my four-and-a-half-year tenure at the Department of Veterans Affairs was to maintain the public website for my program, the Center for Innovation. In addition to regularly updating the content, I developed a number of enhancements to the site's design and functionality.

We went through three different builds while I was there, each of which was initially constructed by a third party. The current iteration was originally produced by Ignition72 on contract, and the work featured here is a combination of their code and mine. Some of my contributions included:

  • Redesigned home page
  • Added support for hyperlinking to specific staff bios (this example links to mine)
  • Redesigned innovation profiles
  • Created new blog with filterable menu
  • Designed resources page to emulate a folder tree

VA GitHub Page

As a side project while with the Center for Innovation, I designed and developed a GitHub landing page for the Department of Veterans Affairs. The page is a gateway to all of VA's open source repositories on GitHub. I followed some stylistic guidance pertaining to the layout and color scheme, but otherwise the design is mine.
